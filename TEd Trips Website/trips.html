Historic Pub Crawl

Drink More Drink

Dublin is a city of pubs... over 1000 of them! Fr jack did try count them all for us even he couldn’t keep up. The highest pub in Dublin is where this trip begins. Johnny Fox’s pub in the Dublin hills. From there its back to the Stags Head in the city. A pub established in 1780 and a prime example of Victorian design. They also serve a great meal. Then were off to the Brazen Head established back in 1198 and blessed by Fr Jack himself on its first day. This was a fond drinking hole of Robert Emmet, Wolfe Tone and Daniel O’Connell though Fr Jack swears it was Daniel O’Donnell!

Mrs Doyles High Tea

Ah Go on

Take a day away and treat yourself ! Mrs Doyle known for her love of tea would be honoured to accompany you and your friends to high tea in one of Dublins most historic hotels. Wearing her best tights, pinny and rollers, Mrs Doyle will introduce you to the high life that is high tea in the Shelbourne Hotel on St Stephens Green. She has the ear of some of the finest pastry chefs outside of craggy island and you are guaranteed a dining experience to remember.

History Tours

Sure that was in the past Ted

Dublin has it all, Vikings, mummies and Ted! We will guide you around trinity College, Dublin Castle and the remnants of the pale. Stroll along the cobbled streets and lanes learning about Dublin’s medieval past. The influence that the famine had in Dublin, the French revolution and the 1916 uprising all told in the ever so slightly florid language of Fr Jack and the patchiness of Fr Dougal’s patter.
